## Every Workaround People Try When Calendly Only Allows 6 Calendars

Professionals facing the reality that Calendly only allows 6 calendars typically attempt several workarounds before seeking permanent solutions. Understanding why these workarounds fail saves time and frustration.

### Workaround 1: Using Calendar Aggregation Services

**What People Try:**
Services like OneCal, CalendarBridge, or SyncThemCalendars sync multiple source calendars into a single master calendar. You connect the master calendar to Calendly, theoretically checking all source calendars while staying within the 6 calendar limit.

**Why It Sounds Good:**
Calendar aggregation appears elegant. You maintain organizational separation while presenting unified availability to scheduling tools.

**Why It Fails in Real Use:**

**Sync Delays Create Double Bookings**
Calendar aggregation requires synchronization between services. This introduces delays ranging from minutes to hours. Someone books a meeting on one calendar. The aggregation service has not synced that event yet. Calendly shows the time as available. Another meeting gets booked. Double booking occurs.

A 2025 study of [calendar synchronization tools](/blog/real-time-calendar-synchronization-explained-2025) found that 78% of users experienced sync delays of 5-15 minutes during peak usage periods. For professionals with back-to-back scheduling, these delays guarantee conflicts.

**Multiple Failure Points Multiply Risk**
Each aggregation service represents a potential failure point. API connection issues, service outages, rate limiting problems, or sync errors disrupt calendar availability. Your scheduling reliability now depends on three systems working perfectly: the aggregation service, Calendly, and your source calendars.

**Hidden Monthly Costs Add Up**
Most calendar aggregation services charge $10-30 monthly. Add this to your existing Calendly subscription of $12-16 per month. You are now paying $22-46 monthly for a workaround instead of a proper solution.

**Manual Conflict Resolution Required**
When the aggregation service encounters conflicts between source calendars, resolution requires manual intervention. You must log in, identify conflicts, and resolve them manually. This defeats the purpose of automated scheduling.

### Workaround 2: Manually Copying Events Between Calendars

**What People Try:**
Manually copy events from multiple calendars into fewer calendars that fit within Calendly's 6 calendar limit. Maintain 10 actual calendars but manually consolidate them into 6 calendars connected to Calendly.

**Why It Sounds Good:**
Manual consolidation requires no third-party services, no additional costs, and gives you complete control over the process.

**Why It Fails in Real Use:**

**Unsustainable Time Investment**
Manual consolidation demands constant attention. Each new event on any source calendar must be manually copied to the appropriate destination calendar. For active professionals, this means dozens of manual copies per week. The time investment quickly becomes unsustainable.

Consider the math. If you receive 5 new calendar events daily across excluded calendars, that is 25 events weekly. At 2 minutes per manual copy, you spend 50 minutes weekly just copying calendar events. That is 43 hours annually wasted on administrative tasks.

**Human Error Guarantees Failures**
Manual processes guarantee errors. You forget to copy an event. You copy it to the wrong calendar. You update an event in one place but forget another. These errors create the double bookings you were trying to prevent.

**Event Updates Become Nightmares**
When an event changes, you must remember to update it in multiple locations. Reschedule a meeting and you need to update both the source calendar and the consolidated calendar. Miss one location and your availability information becomes dangerously inaccurate.

**Zero Scalability**
Manual consolidation cannot scale. As your professional activities expand, the manual workload increases proportionally. You cannot grow your practice without increasing administrative burden.

**What People Try:**

**Why It Sounds Good:**
Strategic exclusion seems pragmatic. Connect your most active calendars to Calendly. Manually check less frequent calendars.

**Why It Fails in Real Use:**

**Double Bookings Become Inevitable**
Excluding calendars from automated conflict checking guarantees eventual double bookings. The question is not whether they will occur but when and how frequently.

**Manual Checking Eliminates Automation Benefits**
Every scheduling request requires manually checking against excluded calendars. You are paying for automation while performing manual verification. The time savings from automated scheduling disappear entirely.

**Persistent Scheduling Anxiety**
Strategic exclusion creates constant low-level anxiety. Every accepted meeting carries uncertainty about whether it conflicts with something on an excluded calendar. This anxiety affects daily operations and decision making.

**Professional Reputation Damage**
When double bookings occur on excluded calendars, clients and colleagues experience the scheduling conflict. They do not care about your calendar management strategy. They experience unprofessionalism.

A 2024 survey of business professionals found that 73% considered double bookings a sign of disorganization and 41% said repeated scheduling conflicts damaged their perception of a professional's competence.
